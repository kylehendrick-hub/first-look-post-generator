# Joshua Baer - X/Twitter Style Guide
*Derived from analysis of highest-performing posts and rewrites*

---

## CORE PRINCIPLES

### 1. Lead with why this person matters
Don't start with the event name. Start with the person's credentials — sell the audience on why they should care.

- **Bad:** "Mike Dodd joined us at How We Win at @StationDC"
- **Good:** "Undersecretary of War Honorable Mike Dodd" / "@ScottKupor used to be one of the biggest baddest VC's at @a16z"

### 2. Show the scene, don't summarize the content
Don't recap talking points. Describe what it felt like to be there. Make readers feel like they missed something.

- **Bad:** "He talked about execution over theory. No science projects."
- **Good:** "The room was totally packed" + a single punchy quote

### 3. Use real quotes, not paraphrased summaries
Pull specific, memorable lines. Real quotes feel like insider access. Paraphrasing feels like a press release.

- **Bad:** "Scott calls it measured risk, deciding the upside is worth it."
- **Good:** "We've told the worst marketing story about why you should work for the government."

### 4. Tell a transformation story
"Was X, now doing Y" is inherently interesting. One sentence, instant narrative.

- "Used to be one of the biggest baddest VC's at @a16z. Now he's running the Office of Personnel Management for the White House."

### 5. Social proof up front
Lead with energy and demand. Show that people showed up.

- "The room was totally packed for..."

### 6. Use full titles for important people
Titles carry weight and signal access/importance.

- "Undersecretary of War Honorable Mike Dodd" — not just "Mike Dodd"

### 7. Casual, confident tone
Write like you talk, not like a press release. No corporate-speak.

- "biggest baddest VCs" — not "one of the most prominent venture capitalists"

---

## STRUCTURE

### Main Tweet Formula
1. **Credentials/social proof** — why this person matters or why the room was packed
2. **Transformation arc** (if applicable) — was X, now doing Y
3. **One punchy real quote**
4. **Tags** — tag the person, their org, the venue, and anyone else relevant
5. **Photo** if you have one

### Reply Thread
Use replies to layer in extra context. Don't cram everything into one post.

- Add a bonus fact about the person ("Grant is the founder of @Epirus, one of the most successful 'single use' startups")
- Use Before/After format for maximum contrast and shareability
- Add your own commentary or hot take on the quote

---

## TAGGING STRATEGY

Tag aggressively for reach. Every tag is an amplification opportunity.

- The person
- Their current organization
- Their previous organization (if relevant to the story)
- The event/venue
- Anyone else mentioned (interviewer, co-panelist)

---

## WHAT TO AVOID

- Starting with the event name ("Joined us at How We Win at...")
- Summarizing talking points like meeting notes ("He talked about X. He said Y. He believes Z.")
- Paraphrasing instead of quoting
- Using only one tag when you could use four
- Generic quotes without context (make sure the reader knows what the quote is about)
- Corporate/formal tone

## FORMAT EXAMPLES

### Event Post (Full)
> .@ScottKupor used to be one of the biggest baddest VC's at @a16z. Now he's running the Office of Personnel Management for the White House and committed to changing the culture of hiring in government. "We've told the worst marketing story about why you should work for the government." @OPM @STATIONDC_HQ
>
> **Reply:** Before: "Get job security for life." After: "Work on the most complex difficult problems in service to your country and you don't need to make a 40 year commitment." @ScottKupor @OPM

### Event Post (Quick Hit)
> The room was totally packed for Undersecretary of War Honorable Mike Dodd at @STATIONDC_HQ. Grant from @RedCellPartners interviewed him about the DOW's priorities and how to support the mission.
>
> **Reply:** Grant is the founder of @Epirus, one of the most successful "single use" startups.

### Business Wisdom (Standalone)
> When you're canceling a service, firing an employee, or terminating a contract, one GOOD reason is all you need and is strongest. Giving a list of reasons just makes it sound like you don't actually have one reason that's good enough by itself.

### Personal Milestone
> As I approach my 50th birthday, I'm finally starting to feel like an adult.

### Quick Overheard (OH) — Enhanced
> OH from @ScottKupor — went from running one of the biggest VC firms in the world (@a16z) to reforming how the government hires. "Constraints force you to prioritize what is actually relevant." @OPM @STATIONDC_HQ
